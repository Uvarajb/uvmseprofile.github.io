Module 2

Network Security

Learning Outcome

In this module we will look at the impact of cyber security on network systems and designs, focusing especially on network vulnerability assessments. We will discuss techniques that can be used to scan, evaluate and in some case mitigate network vulnerabilities. It also will consider the area of vulnerability assessments and explain the various mechanisms and approaches encapsulated in the Vulnerability Assessment Continuum.

Unit 1

This week we will review the history of network security and vulnerabilities and relate it to the modern landscape. We will also discuss some security best practices and review the Cyber Kill Chain model proposed by Lockheed Martin (Hutchins et al, 2011), which was based on a study of contemporary breach approaches

In this unit we shall:
1. Review the history of network security and vulnerabilities.
2. Discuss a best practice approach to security.
3. Review a number of modern scanning and attack approaches.

On completion of this unit you will be able to:
1.Explain the origins of a number of common attacks.
2.Recommend a best practice approach to mitigate attacks.
3.Discuss a number of modern protection techniques.

Collaborative Discussion 2: The Pros and cons of logging – The impact of log4j

Log4j is a tool that is utilized by developers so that they may monitor and record activity that occurs within their software applications. It is essentially to records everything that occurs within an application or system. The practice is known as 'logging,' and it is one of the methods that software developers use in order to monitor potential issues for end users. During November 2021 a critical vulnerability impacting the Log4j2 library was reported, resulting in several fixes and code revisions from the vendor (ANON, 2018)

Pros:
Logs are needed to protect against threats, find intrusions, respond to incidents, do forensics, help with court cases, and follow regulations. They are also used to measure IT performance and monitor and enforce internal policies and procedures. System logs are meant to give you useful information that will help to understand and spot problems that could become threats.
Good log practices can pay off in a big way at every stage of cybersecurity, from helping to profile activity to finding and stopping attacks to, if necessary, doing forensics and fixing problems after a breach. Log retention rules are necessary for enterprises because they guarantee that relevant data will still be accessible in the event that it is required to identify, prevent, or evaluate some future security problem.

Cons: (Andrew,E,2018 & Wistow, 2018)
1. Logging system size can grow
2. Logging information loss and integrity issues
3. Quality of logging data and efforts to log correlation programing
4. Log retention

The Debate – The Future of the internet


The seminar topic discussed various topic to content centric network, Peer-to-Peer overlay-based, Mobility First architecture and the adoption of IPV6.

In Content centric networking, CCN is a revolutionary networking model that emphasizes content distribution above host-to-host communication. This shift from a host-centric to a content-centric architecture has several compelling benefits, including a reduction in network load, low dissemination latency, and improved energy efficiency. It is questionable, however, whether current technology is prepared for the CCN (r)evolution. [Perino, et.all 2011)

Peer-to-Peer: Internet computer and communications settings are more complicated and chaotic than classical distributed systems, without centralized organization and hierarchical control. Peer-to-peer (P2P) network overlays are popular because they enable large-scale data sharing, multimedia distribution, and multicast applications. P2P networks offer a self-organizing, massively scalable, and wide-area routing architecture that combines fault tolerance, load balancing, and locality. This study compares Structured and Unstructured P2P networks.Lua, Et.all 2004)

MobilityFirst Newtork: Mobile platforms and apps are ready to replace the fixed-host/server model that has dominated the Internet since its birth, according to the MobilityFirst project. This anticipated, yet fundamental, transformation gives a rare opportunity to create a next-generation Internet where mobile devices, applications, and the resulting changes in service, trustworthiness, and management are main drivers of a new architecture.

MobilityFirst aims to scale, trust, and use mobile Internet entities. MobilityFirst is a clean-slate project that rethinks end-point names and connection-oriented protocols like TCP. (Rutgers, N.D)

IPV6: IPv6, the latest version of Internet Protocol (IP), will eventually replace IPv4. IP promotes data exchange between networks and defines the address and numbering scheme needed to connect devices to the Internet. IPv6 is not directly compatible with IPv4, but it supports the same Internet services and applications. Many groups are promoting IPv6 globally. IPv6 is needed to expand Internet infrastructure and connect more users and devices. (Internetsociety.org 2016)
